my values

These are the roots beneath everything I do. Not rules — just the truths I keep returning to. They shape how I hold space, how I show up, and what I won't compromise on.

I put the person before the practice. I respect your pace, your boundaries, and your body's wisdom. I will never push you further than your nervous system is ready to go.

I communicate honestly, inclusively, and without jargon. I will not hide behind credentials or spiritual bypassing. All identities are welcome and honoured here.

Your body was never the problem

You were taught to override your instincts, suppress your feelings, and shrink yourself down. But your body has always been your wisest teacher.

Healing is not linear

Some days you'll feel like you've cracked it open. Other days you'll curl up. Both are part of the process. There is no timeline — just the next breath.

"Reclaiming and remembering who you were, before the world told you who you should be."

You belong here

Queer, creative, neurodivergent, messy, loud, quiet — whoever you are, however you show up, there is space for you here.

Community heals

We were never meant to do this alone. Being witnessed from a non-judgmental space changes everything. When we breathe together, something shifts.

Liberation lives in the exhale

Breathwork is a decolonial practice. It's about reclaiming your body, your voice, your agency. Every exhale is an act of letting go — of the stories, the conditioning, the weight that was never yours to carry.

Trust the body's wisdom

Your nervous system holds the answers. Not the self-help books, not the guru. When you slow down enough to listen, your body will tell you exactly what it needs.

No shame, no scarcity

I won't use fear, urgency, or pain points to get you through the door. This space is built on integrity, accessibility, and genuine care.

What I won't do

  • Use shame to motivate
  • Create false scarcity
  • Agitate pain points for profit
  • Use misleading or confusing language
  • Bypass your boundaries — ever

I welcome you to hold me accountable. This is a living practice, not a performance.

You don't need to be fixed.
You need to be felt.
